Ships of the Vikings

Warships (Langskip):
A warship is narrower, longer, and shallower than a knörr, and is powered by oars and the sail. The warship is completely open and is built for speed and maneuverability. The crew's shields may be be spread along the sides of the ship. This provided some protection against enemy arrows, wind, and waves.
Merchant Ships (Knörr):
In contrast, a knörr is partially enclosed and powered primarily by sail. Cargo carrying capability is the primary concern. It is able to be operated by a smaller crew compared to the langskip. Vikings used the ship to trade goods wool, timber, wheat, furs and pelts, armour, slaves, honey, and weapons.
