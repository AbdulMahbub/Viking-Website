The Vikings were the people who lived from the 8th century to the 11th century in Scandinavia. Today that land is known as Denmark, Norway, and Sweden. The Vikings are known for their tales of pillaging villages and being great warriors.The Vikings loved to sail and explore so much that they would even discover new lands on their journeys.

The Vikings did not speak Danish, Norwegian, or Swedish. They spoke a language called Old Norse. And because the Vikings loved to travel and met new people, The Danes while the Swedes spoke Old East Norse, and the Norwegians spoke Old West Norse. Their language slowly spread into other societies because of their travels. Languages such as English and Russian still have words that originate from the Viking language.
